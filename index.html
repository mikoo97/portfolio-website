// 01 — About

Who I Am

I'm a project coordinator, social researcher, and policy advocate with 4+ years leading data-driven programmes across public health, climate, and civil society sectors in Indonesia.

My background in Theology (UKSW, GPA 3.74) is not incidental — it grounds my research in ethics, community trust, and participatory approaches. I believe data is only as powerful as the communities that generate it and the policies that respond to it.

At LaporCovid-19, I coordinated national-scale citizen reporting at a moment of genuine public health crisis. At Lapor Iklim, I carried that commitment into climate advocacy. My work has reached the floors of national media and the desks of government ministers.

// 07 — Notes

Thoughts & Reflections

Public Health · Advocacy
What Citizen Data Can (and Cannot) Do
Citizen-generated data is powerful precisely because it captures what official channels miss. But it also carries its own biases, gaps, and risks. At LaporCovid-19, we learned this the hard way — and it made our research better.
Reflections from 2021–2024
Climate · Community
Climate Justice Starts with Listening
The communities most vulnerable to climate change are often those least heard in policy rooms. My work at Lapor Iklim reinforced a simple truth: good climate policy requires proximity to affected communities, not just data models.
Reflections from 2024–2025
Research · Method
Why Mixed Methods Still Matter
In an era of big data, there's pressure to reduce everything to numbers. But numbers without context are stories without meaning. Mixed-methods research — combining the quantitative with the qualitative — remains the most honest approach to complex social problems.
Ongoing reflection
